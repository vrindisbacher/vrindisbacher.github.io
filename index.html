About Me

Hi there! I am a recent graduate of Boston University's Computer Science program, and currently a Software Engineer based out of Austin, Texas. Next year, I hope to begin my PhD in the area of Programming Languages.

I spent most of my time in high school racing bicycles at a high level. My palmares include podiums at National Championships and membership of the Canadian National Team. In 2018, I began my Bachelor's degree in Health Science, but switched to Computer Science and wrote code for the first time after a Freshman Internship at CCNS, a high performance coaching and testing company. I took my first Introduction to Programming Languages class and fell in love with the topic. This led me to aggressively pursue a curriculum of graduate courses, the most notable being a class on Formal Methods.

Currently, I am conducting Research on Formally Verifying Algorithms for Differential Privacy, with Professor Marco Gaboardi and Professor Arthur Azevedo De Amorim. My research interests are mainly in the area of Formal Verification, but I am also interested in type theory, language semantics, and probabilistic programming. My goal is to work in the cross section of engineering and theory.

Research Interests

Writing correct code is hard. When I first learned about Formal Methods I believed that all software should be verified. Unfortunately, the overhead of doing mechanized mathematical proofs is too large to realistically be able to accomplish such an expansive goal. Thus, I have a keen interest on lowering the barriers for engineers to ensure that productionalized code is correct.

In my future research, I aim to work in the cross section of theory and engineering. I believe that great engineering is driven by theory, but for systems to be useful, the theory must be abstracted away. Thus, I am heavily focused on work that can have immediate impact on industrial applications. My work on Differential Privacy is an example of this.
